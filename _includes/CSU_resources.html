United States Warning Centers

  • National Hurricane Center – United States operational warning center for the North Atlantic and Northeast Pacific (to 140°W).
  • Central Pacific Hurricane Center – United States operational warning center for the North Central Pacific (from 140°W-180°).
  • Joint Typhoon Warning Center – United States warning center for the Northwest Pacific, the North Indian Ocean and the Southern Hemisphere.

Model Guidance

  • NCAR Tropical Cyclone Guidance Project – Real-time operational model output whenever tropical cyclones are active anywhere around the globe.
  • Tropical Tidbits – Global forecast model output and real-time analysis tools.
  • SUNY-Albany (Brian Tang) – Operational guidance as well as model trends and preliminary verification for active North Atlantic and Northeast Pacific tropical cyclones.

Satellite Analysis

  • Cooperative Institute for Research in the Atmosphere – Extensive suite of satellite analysis tools.
  • Naval Research Lab – Wide array of satellite tools.
  • Cooperative Institute for Meteorological Satellite Studies – Vast array of satellite platforms – also allows the ability to overlay satellite-analyzed shear over forecast model tracks of storms

Blogs

  • Washington Post’s Capital Weather Gang – Frequent blogs about tropical cyclones, especially during the Northern Hemisphere hurricane season.
  • Brian McNoldy (Univ. of Miami) – Blogs on Atlantic tropical cyclones throughout the hurricane season.
  • Weather Underground (Jeff Masters and Bob Henson) – Frequent blogs about tropical cyclones when they are active anywhere around the globe.

Global Tropical Cyclone Statistics

  • Ryan Maue – Real-time global Accumulated Cyclone Energy tabulations – along with plots of historical ACE and hurricane frequency.
